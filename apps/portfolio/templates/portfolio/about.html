I was a recent university graduate who just wanted to learn how to code in python. Coding Dojo made me fall in love completely with programming and the skills I learned have opened me up to opportunities that most university students are unable to participate in.
Coding Dojo is not easy, it is very fast-paced and requires long days (like 12-16 hour days) and a lot of effort to learn everything.
They also practice GAP LEARNING, intentionally leaving pieces of code out to force you to learn how to learn on your own (like you will in the real world).
I was told they don't just train you to become a programmer, they train you to be self-sufficient.
I graduated February 2018 and have learned three full stacks (Python, Ruby, and Mean), I also developed the ability to self-learn new languages, and made life-long friends.
